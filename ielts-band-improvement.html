The brutal truth: why you stay stuck

Band stuck hoy because your practice is not “measurable”. Apni hoyto 2 ghonta poren — but wrong target e maren. Band improvement always comes from:

  • Accuracy discipline (not motivation)
  • Timing control (not long study hours)
  • Weakness repeat (not random full tests)
  • Computer-based exam flow (Bangladesh e almost keu train kore na)

Band map: what each band looks like

Band 5 → “bujhi, but score ashe na”

  • Reading e keyword match kore — meaning miss
  • Listening e panic → ekta miss, pore shob miss
  • Writing e idea thake, structure + clarity thake na
  • Speaking e chesta kore, but control thake na

Band 6 → “stable, but jump hoy na”

  • Too many small mistakes (spelling, grammar, logic)
  • Timing “almost” — but last 2 passage / last paragraph break
  • Speaking e ideas ase, but Part 3 logic weak
  • Writing e coherence thake, but Task response incomplete

Band 7 → “control + consistency”

  • Meaning-first reading, not line-first reading
  • Listening e prediction + trap control
  • Writing e clear position + clean structure + precise examples
  • Speaking e smooth expansion + natural grammar

Real secret: “less mistakes”

Band 7 is not fancy English. It’s fewer errors under pressure. That’s it. That’s the game.

Reading: Band 5→6→7 fix list (Bangladesh focused)

Reading e Band jump mostly ashe timing + question logic theke. Vocabulary kom thakle o 6+ possible — but you must stop doing “line-by-line translate”.

  • Band 5→6: stop keyword hunting, start meaning hunting (1 line = 1 idea)
  • Band 6→7: inference + writer’s claim identify (not only facts)
  • Timing rule: Passage 1 (15m), Passage 2 (20m), Passage 3 (25m)
  • Trap rule: True/False/Not Given = writer said? not “I think”

Common BD leak

“Bujhte hobe” pressure → Bangla translate → time shesh.

Fix (simple)

Skim first (topic), then scan with purpose (question type).

Practice: Reading

Listening: band jump is prediction + recovery

Listening e most marks jai attention loss e. You don’t need perfect English. You need perfect process.

  • Band 5→6: spelling + numbers + dates control (free marks)
  • Band 6→7: prediction speed + paraphrase awareness
  • Recovery: miss korle instantly next question (no panic)
  • Computer-based: typing + navigation pressure practice
Practice: Listening

Writing: Band 6 is structure. Band 7 is clarity.

Writing e Band stuck hoy because people write long but unclear. Examiner wants: position clear, paragraphs clean, examples relevant.

  • Band 5→6: fixed structure + topic sentence + simple cohesion
  • Band 6→7: strong position + precise example + fewer grammar slips
  • Task 2 rule: answer the question type (advantages ≠ causes)
  • Task 1 rule: overview first, details later (no story)
Practice: Writing

Speaking: Band 7 is “control under pressure”

Speaking e problem vocabulary na. Problem is freeze + repeat + weak Part 3 logic.

  • Band 5→6: 1 idea → example → reason → feeling (repeat korben na)
  • Band 6→7: Part 3 opinion + justification + contrast (simple words OK)
  • Fluency: natural pause, not dead stop
  • Pronunciation: clarity > accent
Practice: Speaking

7-day band fix plan (do this, not random mocks)

Apni jodi quick improvement chan, “full mock everyday” is not the plan. Targeted practice is the plan.

Day 1–2: Accuracy Reset

  • Listening: spelling + numbers drills
  • Reading: TFNG + Matching headings only
  • Writing: 1 Task 2 structure (no fancy)

Day 3–4: Timing Control

  • Reading: strict 15/20/25 timing
  • Listening: read-ahead speed + recovery
  • Speaking: Part 2 1-minute plan + 2-minute talk

Day 5–6: Exam Simulation

  • Computer-based flow practice
  • One full mock (then review mistakes properly)
  • Fix top 10 repeated errors

Day 7: Stabilize

  • Only weak modules
  • Short, repeatable drills
  • No burnout

Band jump is boring work done consistently. Not motivation speeches.
