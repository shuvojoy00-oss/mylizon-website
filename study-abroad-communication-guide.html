Abroad Communication: 16 Real-Life Phases

IELTS score thaklei real-life communication ready hoye jawa jay na. Abroad e giye student-ra je real situation-gulo face kore — immigration, airport, room, university, shop, doctor, job, boss, customer, pressure — ei page ta shegulo niye. Read this page like a study guide. Learn the questions, understand the answers, and practice the survival lines.

Phase 1

Immigration

First official conversation after landing.

What happens here

You land, stand in line, face an immigration officer, and answer direct questions. The officer usually speaks short, clearly, but sometimes quickly. Ekhane beshi kotha bola lagbe na. Clear, short, confident answer dite hobe.

Why Bangladeshi students struggle here

  • Nervous hoye extra kotha bole.
  • Question fully na bujhe random answer dey.
  • University name, course name, address confidently bolte pare na.
  • “Repeat please” bolte lojja pay.

Possible questions

Why are you here?

Which university are you going to?

What course are you studying?

How long will you stay?

Where will you stay?

Who is funding your studies?

Can I see your documents?

Is this your first time here?

When does your course start?

Do you have your accommodation details?

Strong short answers

I’m here to study at [University Name].

I’m enrolled in a [Bachelor’s / Master’s] program in [Subject].

My course duration is [X] years.

I’ll stay at [dorm / address].

My family is supporting me financially.

Yes, sure. Here are my documents.

Yes, this is my first time here.

My course starts on [date / month].

Survival lines

  • Sorry, could you repeat that?
  • Could you please speak a bit slower?
  • I didn’t understand the last part.
  • Let me show you my documents.

Important words

  • Enrolled = officially admitted
  • Duration = course time
  • Funding = who pays
  • Accommodation = where you stay

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t joke with immigration officer.
  • Don’t give a long story.
  • Don’t guess if you didn’t hear.
  • Don’t speak too fast because of nervousness.
Phase 2

Airport Navigation

Baggage, exits, directions, and asking for help.

What happens here

After immigration, you find baggage claim, trolley, exit, washroom, transport area, or help desk. This is often the first time you follow English signs and spoken directions in a real environment.

Why students struggle here

  • Direction words bujhte problem hoy.
  • Accent + airport noise = extra confusion.
  • One question-er moddhe 2-3 instruction thake.

Possible questions / situations

Excuse me, where is the baggage claim?

Where can I get a trolley?

Which way is the exit?

I can’t find my luggage. Can you help me?

Where is the taxi / rideshare area?

Can you show me this place on the map?

Useful answers / responses

It’s straight ahead, then turn left.

The trolleys are near the entrance.

Follow the signs on your right.

Please go to the baggage service desk.

The pickup area is outside Terminal 2.

Survival lines

  • Sorry, I’m a bit confused.
  • Can you say that once more?
  • Can you show me on the sign?
  • Is it far from here?

Important words

  • Straight ahead = directly forward
  • Turn left / right = bam / dan
  • Baggage claim = luggage collection area
  • Terminal = airport section

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t act like you understood when you didn’t.
  • Don’t panic if you feel lost.
  • Don’t ask long questions. Keep them simple.
Phase 3

Transport (Taxi / Uber / Pickup)

Confirm ride, destination, route, and payment.

What happens here

After leaving the airport, you usually use Uber, taxi, airport pickup, or a friend’s ride. You must confirm name, destination, route, and payment. Showing the address on your phone is normal and smart.

Possible questions

Are you [Your Name]?

Where are you going?

Is this the correct address?

Do you want me to drop you off here?

Card or cash?

Is this your first time here?

Why students struggle here

  • Address clearly bolte pare na.
  • Driver-er natural speed bujhte time lage.
  • Payment, route, traffic niye quick response dite hoy.

Strong short answers

Yes, that’s me.

I’m going to this address. Let me show you.

Yes, this is correct.

Please stop near the main entrance.

I’ll pay by card.

Yes, I arrived today.

Survival lines

  • Let me show you the location.
  • Sorry, I didn’t catch that.
  • How long will it take?
  • Please drop me off here.

Important words

  • Drop off = namano
  • Traffic is heavy = jam beshi
  • It’ll take a while = time lagbe

Mistakes to avoid

  • Always confirm destination before moving.
  • Don’t say “yes” blindly if unsure.
  • Don’t depend only on pronunciation; show the address too.
Phase 4

Accommodation (Dorm / Landlord / Roommates)

Check in, ask rules, solve room issues, and introduce yourself.

What happens here

You arrive at your dorm, apartment, or shared house. You check in, show ID, understand rent and rules, ask about WiFi and bills, and maybe meet roommates on the same day.

Possible situations / questions

Hi, I’m here to check in.

Can I see your ID?

What is the WiFi password?

Are bills included in the rent?

There’s a problem with the water / heating.

Hi, I’m [Name]. Nice to meet you.

What time is quiet time?

Where should I keep the trash?

Why students struggle here

  • Bill, deposit, rent, maintenance-er words bujhte pare na.
  • Room issue bolte gie specific hote pare na.
  • Roommate-der sathe first conversation awkward hoy.

Strong short answers

I have a booking under [Your Name].

Sure, here is my ID.

Yes, I’d like to know the WiFi password.

There’s a problem with the water in my room.

I usually sleep early, so I prefer a quiet environment.

I’m from Bangladesh. Nice to meet you.

Survival lines

  • Sorry, could you explain that again?
  • What do you mean by included?
  • Can someone check this, please?
  • I’m not sure I understood the rule.

Important words

  • Included = rent-er moddhe ache
  • Shared = onoder sathe use korte hobe
  • Maintenance = repair / service
  • Deposit = security money

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t assume everything is included in rent.
  • Don’t stay silent if there’s a room problem.
  • Don’t be rude with roommates from day one.
Phase 5

Basic Survival (Shopping / Grocery / Store)

Finding items, asking staff, talking to cashiers, and paying.

What happens here

You go to grocery stores, mini markets, big retail shops, or pharmacies. You ask where items are, check prices, understand offers, and complete payment.

Common questions / situations

Excuse me, where can I find rice?

Which aisle is the cooking oil in?

How much is this?

Is there any discount on this?

Do you need a bag?

Card or cash?

Can you help me with self-checkout?

Why students struggle here

  • Aisle, receipt, contactless, discount-er moto word confuse kore.
  • Cashier ra onek shomoy short form e kotha bole.
  • Fast question shune answer dite deri hoy.

Strong short answers

I’m looking for rice / milk / eggs.

Sorry, which aisle was that?

Yes, please. I need a bag.

No, thank you. I’m fine.

I’ll pay by card.

Could you help me with this machine?

Survival lines

  • Can you show me where it is?
  • I didn’t catch that.
  • Is there a cheaper option?
  • I’m looking for this item.

Important words

  • Aisle = product row
  • Receipt = bill copy
  • Contactless = tap payment
  • Discount = reduced price

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t be shy to point at the item name on your phone.
  • Don’t stand silently when self-checkout gets stuck.
  • Don’t overcomplicate your question.
Phase 6

Setup Essentials (SIM / Bank / Services)

Phone, mobile data, bank account, cards, and apps.

What happens here

First week-e student usually SIM card ney, bank account khole, mobile app setup kore, and basic service activate kore. Ekhane staff often step-by-step instruction dey.

Common questions

Hi, I need a SIM card.

Prepaid or postpaid?

Which plan is good for students?

Can I see your ID?

I want to open a bank account.

Do you have your passport and documents?

When will I get my card?

Is there an app for this?

Why students struggle here

  • Instruction fast hole miss kore.
  • Form fill-up er language bujhte trouble hoy.
  • Next step jante chay, but proper English line pay na.

Strong short answers

Prepaid, please.

Which plan do you recommend for a student?

Sure, here is my ID.

Yes, I have all the required documents.

Can you help me activate it?

When will the card arrive?

Survival lines

  • I’m not familiar with this process.
  • Can you guide me step by step?
  • Could you explain that again slowly?
  • What should I do next?

Important words

  • Prepaid = pay first
  • Postpaid = pay later
  • Activate = chalu kora
  • Required documents = dorkar kagoj

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t pretend you understood the whole process.
  • Don’t leave without knowing what happens next.
  • Don’t lose your forms or ID papers.
Phase 7

University Start (Admin / Class / Teacher)

First contact with university staff, lecturers, and class systems.

What happens here

You go to admin office, ask about class schedule, find rooms, attend lectures, and talk to teachers after or during class. This is where many students realize IELTS classroom English and real university English are not the same.

Common questions / situations

Hi, I’m a new student. I need some guidance.

Where is Room 204?

Hello, I just joined this class.

Could you explain that again?

Can I ask a question?

I have a question about today’s lecture.

When is the assignment due?

How do I access the student portal?

Why students struggle here

  • Lecture English natural and fast hoy.
  • Teacher-er question korte hesitation thake.
  • Deadline, submit, portal, attendance-er system initially unfamiliar lage.

Strong short answers

I’d like to know my class schedule.

I didn’t understand that part. Could you explain it again?

What does this term mean?

When do we need to submit this?

I’m a new student, so I’m still learning the system.

Survival lines

  • Can you give an example?
  • I’m a bit confused about this.
  • Could you repeat the instruction?
  • Can you tell me where I can find this information?

Important words

  • Assignment = kaj
  • Deadline = last date
  • Submit = joma dewa
  • Portal = online student system

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t stay silent when confused.
  • Don’t miss instructions because of fear.
  • Don’t wait too long to talk to admin or teacher.
Phase 8

Social Interaction (Friends / Classmates)

Introductions, small talk, joining groups, and casual connection.

What happens here

You meet classmates, people in accommodation, group members, and other students. This is where real daily English starts. Perfect grammar lagbe na. Conversation alive rakhte hobe.

Common questions / situations

Hi, I’m [Your Name]. Nice to meet you.

What’s your name?

Where are you from?

What are you studying?

How are you finding the class?

Can I join you guys?

Do you want to study together sometime?

Why students struggle here

  • First line bolar confidence thake na.
  • English perfect korte giye silent hoye jay.
  • Short reaction expressions jane na.

Strong short answers

I’m from Bangladesh.

I’m studying [Subject].

It’s a bit challenging, but interesting.

Sure, I’d love to join.

That sounds good.

Survival lines

  • That’s interesting.
  • Really?
  • Oh, I see.
  • Nice talking to you.

Important words

  • What’s up? = casual hello
  • Not bad = motamoti bhalo
  • Kind of = ekto / somewhat

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t wait for others to always start first.
  • Don’t aim for impressive English. Aim for connection.
  • Don’t disappear after one awkward moment.
Phase 9

Academic Communication (Discussion / Group Work / Opinion)

Understanding ideas and responding in a smart, simple way.

What happens here

Classes become more discussion-based. You need to ask for clarification, give opinions, agree, disagree politely, and work in a group. This phase is less about survival and more about expression.

Common questions / situations

Could you explain this again?

What does this term mean?

Can you give an example?

I think this point is important because…

I agree with you.

I see your point, but I think…

Should we do it this way?

Why students struggle here

  • Opinion dite giye line build korte pare na.
  • Polite disagreement-er expression jane na.
  • Group work e role clearly bole na.

Strong short answers

I think this idea is useful because it is practical.

That makes sense to me.

I understand your point, but I have a different view.

I can do this part of the task.

When do we need to submit it?

Survival lines

  • Let me check if I got this right.
  • Can you explain it in a simple way?
  • What do you think?
  • Should we divide the work?

Important words

  • Point = idea
  • Argument = reason / viewpoint
  • Discuss = alochona kora
  • Reference = source

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t memorize fake “smart” English.
  • Don’t disagree rudely.
  • Don’t avoid group contribution.
Phase 10

Daily Life Problems (Internet / Rent / Calls / Complaints)

Explaining problems clearly and asking for a solution.

What happens here

Internet stops, heating fails, card doesn’t work, service gets delayed, or a room issue appears. Then you need to call, explain, wait, follow instructions, and ask when it will be fixed.

Common questions / situations

Hi, my internet is not working.

What’s the issue?

The connection is very slow.

When will it be fixed?

There is a problem in my room.

Could you please check it?

Why students struggle here

  • Problem specific kore bolte pare na.
  • Phone call e listening aro hard hoy.
  • Emotionally react kore, but clear line bole na.

Strong short answers

My internet is not working at all.

The heating in my room is not working.

I’ve had this issue for two days.

Could you tell me when it will be fixed?

Can you send someone to check it?

Survival lines

  • Let me explain clearly.
  • I’m having trouble with my service.
  • Could you repeat that slowly?
  • I need help with this issue.

Important words

  • Issue = problem
  • Fix = thik kora
  • Technician = repair person
  • Connection = internet/service line

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t only say “It’s bad.” Be specific.
  • Don’t forget to ask for timeline.
  • Don’t get emotional before explaining the issue.
Phase 11

Job Search (Asking / Applying / Interview Talk)

Finding openings, speaking to managers, and giving a basic self-introduction.

What happens here

You ask for job openings, leave a CV, talk to the manager, answer basic questions, and maybe get called for an interview. Ekhane grammar theke beshi important hocche confidence + clarity + attitude.

Common questions / situations

Hi, are there any job openings?

Can I speak to the manager?

Tell me about yourself.

When can you work?

Do you have any experience?

What kind of work are you looking for?

Can you work weekends?

Why students struggle here

  • “Tell me about yourself” line-e atke jay.
  • Availability clear korte pare na.
  • No experience hole awkward feel kore.

Strong short answers

I’m a student and I’m looking for a part-time job.

I’m available in the evenings and on weekends.

I have some experience / I don’t have experience, but I learn quickly.

I’m hardworking, reliable, and willing to learn.

Here is my CV.

Survival lines

  • I’m interested in this role.
  • Could you tell me more about the job?
  • Thank you, I’ll wait for your call.
  • I can start from next week.

Important words

  • Opening = job khali ache
  • Shift = work time slot
  • Availability = when you can work
  • Reliable = dependable

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t say “I can do anything” without thinking.
  • Don’t look too unsure about your schedule.
  • Don’t speak negatively about your lack of experience.
Phase 12

Part-Time Job (Restaurant Work)

Taking orders, talking to customers, fixing mistakes, and handling pressure.

What happens here

In restaurant work, English gets fast and practical. Customer order, kitchen pressure, supervisor instruction, complaint, time — sob kichu ekshathe ashbe. Calm thaka is part of communication skill.

Common questions / situations

Hi, what would you like to order?

Sorry, could you repeat that?

So, one burger and a coke, right?

How long will it take?

This is not what I ordered.

I need help with this order.

Can you show me how to do this?

Why students struggle here

  • Fast customer speech bujhte tough lage.
  • Pressure-e order confirm na kore mistake hoy.
  • Complaint handle korte nervous hoy.

Strong short answers

Sorry, could you say that again?

So, one chicken burger and a coke, right?

It will take around 10 minutes.

I’m really sorry. Let me fix this for you.

One moment, please.

Survival lines

  • Let me check that for you.
  • I’ll be right back.
  • Can you please wait a moment?
  • I need help with this table/order.

Important words

  • Takeaway = niye jawa
  • Dine-in = ekhane bose khaoa
  • Combo = combined offer
  • Order = customer request

Mistakes to avoid

  • Never assume the order. Confirm it.
  • Don’t argue with the customer.
  • Don’t panic if you need help. Call supervisor fast.
Phase 13

Other Jobs (Warehouse / Car Wash / Delivery / Driving)

Following instructions, confirming tasks, and communicating location or timing.

What happens here

These jobs may have less conversation than restaurant work, but instruction understanding is critical. Wrong place, wrong box, wrong address, or wrong timing means trouble.

Common questions / situations

Put these boxes over there.

Do you want me to put all of these here?

Sorry, can you show me once?

I need a full wash.

Hi, I’m outside your location. Can you guide me?

I can’t find the address.

What should I do next?

Why students struggle here

  • Instruction confirm na kore assume kore fele.
  • Location-related English weak thake.
  • Boss-er short command-gulo bujhte time lage.

Strong short answers

Okay. Where exactly should I put them?

Do you want me to do all of these now?

It will take around 20 minutes.

Can you send the location again?

I’ve completed the task. What next?

Survival lines

  • Let me confirm.
  • Can you show me once?
  • I’ll do it now.
  • I’m outside the location.

Important words

  • Load / unload = uthano / namano
  • Drop-off = delivery point
  • Shift = work time
  • Route = path

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t act before understanding the instruction.
  • Don’t stay silent if you can’t find the location.
  • Don’t say “okay” just to finish the conversation.
Phase 14

Workplace Communication (Coworkers / Boss / Daily Talk)

Short natural English, slang, quick updates, and teamwork talk.

What happens here

Once you settle into the job, communication becomes shorter and more natural. Coworkers use casual English, jokes, short reactions, and quick task updates. This phase is about staying active in the workplace.

Common questions / situations

How’s it going?

Busy day, huh?

Can you help me with this?

Do you need any help?

Hurry up!

That was my mistake. I’ll fix it.

See you tomorrow.

Why students struggle here

  • Slang shune confused hoy.
  • Quick reply er habit thake na.
  • Pressure moment-e natural line ber hoy na.

Strong short answers

Good, how about you?

Yeah, it’s really busy today.

Sure, I can help.

I’m on it.

Sorry, that was my mistake. I’ll fix it.

Survival lines

  • Give me a second.
  • I’m working on it.
  • Got it.
  • No problem.

Important words

  • No worries = it’s okay
  • Hang on = wait a bit
  • Gonna = going to
  • Wanna = want to

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t over-explain every small thing.
  • Don’t freeze when someone speaks casually.
  • Don’t ignore team conversation all day.
Phase 15

Real-Life Stress Situations (Misunderstanding / Rude People / Pressure)

Staying calm when English is fast, unclear, or emotionally difficult.

What happens here

Real world sob shomoy polite hoy na. Someone may speak quickly, look annoyed, repeat less, or sound rude. Your goal is not to win the moment. Your goal is to understand, stay calm, and respond properly.

Common situations

Sorry, I didn’t understand.

Could you speak a bit slower, please?

Let me check if I understood correctly…

I think there was a misunderstanding.

What do you mean exactly?

I’m a bit confused. Can you guide me?

Why students struggle here

  • Rude tone-ke personal ney.
  • Confusion hole blank hoye jay.
  • Same question abar korte embarrassment feel kore.

Best response style

I’m trying to understand. Could you explain that again?

Let me explain one more time.

Okay, I understand now. Thank you.

Could you please slow down a little?

Survival lines

  • Give me a moment.
  • Let me try again.
  • I understand now.
  • Can you clarify that?

Important words / tones

  • Come on = hurry / frustration
  • Listen = attention dao
  • Misunderstanding = wrong understanding
  • Clarify = make clear

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t respond with anger first.
  • Don’t say “okay” if you are still confused.
  • Don’t shut down mentally after one bad interaction.
Phase 16

Health / Emergency (Doctor / Pharmacy)

Explaining symptoms, receiving instructions, and asking the right follow-up questions.

What happens here

You may get sick, visit a doctor, go to a clinic, talk to reception, or buy medicine from a pharmacy. In health situations, simple and clear English matters more than fancy English.

Common questions / situations

Hi, I’d like to see a doctor.

What’s the problem?

I have a headache / fever / sore throat.

I’ve had this for two days.

It hurts here.

How often should I take this medicine?

I need help. It’s urgent.

Why students struggle here

  • Symptom describe korar English weak thake.
  • Medicine instruction miss kore.
  • Emergency moment-e brain fast kaaj kore na.

Strong short answers

I have had a fever since yesterday.

It hurts here.

Could you explain the instructions again?

How many times a day should I take this?

I’m not feeling well.

Survival lines

  • It started yesterday.
  • What should I do now?
  • Can you write that down for me?
  • I need urgent help.

Important words

  • Prescription = doctor-er likha medicine
  • Twice a day = dine 2 bar
  • Before / after meals = khabar age / pore
  • Urgent = immediate need

Mistakes to avoid

  • Don’t hide symptoms because of language fear.
  • Don’t leave without understanding medicine instructions.
  • Don’t act “fine” if it is actually urgent.
Growth Stage

2 to 6 Months: Real Communication Growth

From survival English to real confidence, opinion, and connection.

What changes in this stage

Now survival is not the main issue. The next level is confidence, fluency, clearer opinions, deeper conversations, and less fear. This is where students should stop trying to sound perfect and start sounding natural.

Useful real-life lines

I think this is a good idea because…

What I mean is…

I completely agree with you.

I understand your point, but I see it differently.

What do you think about this?

In my country, it’s a bit different.

It was nice talking to you. Let’s stay in touch.

What students should do in this stage

  • Every day kichu na kichu English-e bolo.
  • Opinion-based conversation practice koro.
  • Short reaction theke full idea porjonto build koro.
  • Accent fear komao by listening more.
  • Real conversation e mistake holeo continue koro.

Confidence answers

I’m getting more comfortable now.

I can express myself better than before.

I still make mistakes, but I can communicate.

I’m learning to speak more naturally.

Survival lines that become fluency lines

  • Let me think for a second.
  • That’s a good question.
  • I see your point.
  • That makes sense.

Important words

  • Fair enough = thik ache, bujhlam
  • From my perspective = amar dike theke
  • Makes sense = logical lage

Biggest mistake in this stage

  • Still waiting to become “perfect” before speaking.
  • Only listening, not responding.
  • Staying only with your own language group all the time.
