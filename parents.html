Common Questions Parents Have

Safety & Wellbeing

Understanding the environment, support systems, and living conditions that ensure a student's security abroad.

Education Quality

Evaluating institution rankings, teaching methods, and the recognition of degrees internationally.

Living Expenses

Estimating day-to-day costs, including food, transportation, utilities, and managing a sustainable monthly budget.

Accommodation

Exploring housing options, from on-campus dormitories to off-campus apartments and homestays.

Career Opportunities

Assessing internships, part-time work availability, and industry connections provided by the university.

Future Planning

Looking at long-term outcomes, including post-graduation pathways and global networking possibilities.

Questions Parents Often Ask

Costs vary significantly depending on destination, institution, lifestyle, and individual circumstances.

Safety depends on many factors, including location, preparation, and support systems.

Rules differ by country and may change over time.

Universities often consider multiple aspects of a student's profile.

Post-study opportunities vary depending on destination, program, and individual goals.

Every Student Journey Is Different

Two students with similar goals may require very different pathways.

Understanding the student's profile often helps identify realistic next steps.

Academic Background

Previous degrees, grades, and subjects heavily influence which universities are accessible.

English Level

Proficiency scores dictate whether direct entry or preparatory courses are required.

Study Gap

Time spent away from formal education must be accounted for and explained during applications.

Budget

Financial parameters directly shape the shortlist of countries and institutions.

Career Goals

Long-term aspirations determine the most relevant programs and degree levels.

Timeline

Intake dates and visa processing times dictate when applications need to be submitted.
