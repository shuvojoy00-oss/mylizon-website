The Evolution of the Modern Shipping Container

A. The global economy would be non-existent without a simple box. Before its invention, loading was labor-intensive: "Break-bulk" shipping involved men manually hauling barrels. Much space was wasted.

B. The revolution began with Malcolm McLean, a former truck driver, who was frustrated by the slow process. In 1956, his tanker, the Ideal X, carried 58 containers. This proved loading costs could be reduced by over 90%, from $5.86 per ton to just $0.16.

C. Lack of uniformity was a hurdle. It wasn't until the late 1960s that the ISO established standard dimensions. This created an intermodal network.

D. Lower costs allowed manufacturing to be outsourced to countries with lower labor costs. This led to the decline of traditional industrial hubs in the West. Ports without deep-water terminals faced obsolescence.

E. Large ships burn fuel oil, causing emissions. Security is a concern; only a small percentage are physically inspected, allowing for smuggling.

F. Trade imbalances mean thousands of empty boxes pile up because it is cheaper to leave them than ship them back. This led to architectural solutions like container housing.

Questions 1-7: TFNG

1. "Break-bulk" was efficient.
English: The text says it was "labor-intensive," which is the opposite of efficient. বাংলা: প্যাসেজে বলা হয়েছে এটি অনেক পরিশ্রমের এবং ঝামেলার কাজ ছিল, তাই এটি দক্ষ বা efficient ছিল না।
2. McLean's job helped him.
English: His experience as a truck driver influenced his vision. বাংলা: তার ট্রাক ড্রাইভার হিসেবে অভিজ্ঞতা তাকে এই নতুন পদ্ধতি চিন্তা করতে সাহায্য করেছিল।
3. Cost savings were over 50%.
English: Costs dropped by 90%, which is more than 50%. বাংলা: খরচ ৯০% কমেছে, যা ৫০% এর চেয়ে অনেক বেশি।
