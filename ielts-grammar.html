Why grammar fails in real IELTS exams

Bangladeshi students often say: “Grammar jani, but writing/speaking e bhul hoy.” This happens because IELTS tests live usage, not theory.

  • Accuracy drops under time pressure
  • Tense confusion in Task 2 & Speaking
  • Sentence control loss
  • Overthinking rules

Accuracy: the only grammar thing IELTS cares about

IELTS does not reward “advanced grammar” if it’s wrong. Simple + correct beats complex + broken.

  • Subject–verb agreement
  • Correct tense choice
  • Clean sentence endings
  • No repeated basic mistakes

Grammar control under pressure

Anyone can write correct grammar slowly. IELTS checks if you can maintain it while thinking.

  • One idea = one controlled sentence
  • No mid-sentence panic
  • Structure first, grammar follows

Tense: where most bands die

Tense mistakes don’t look serious — but they silently pull bands down.

  • Task 1: past vs present confusion
  • Task 2: mixed tense paragraphs
  • Speaking: jumping timelines

Sentence structure: stop writing long, broken lines

IELTS prefers clarity over length. Long sentences with errors = band damage.

  • Controlled compound sentences
  • Safe complex sentences
  • Clear punctuation

Overthinking rules kills grammar

Many students think: “Eta passive? conditional? relative?” IELTS doesn’t care what it’s called.

  • Use what feels controlled
  • Avoid risky patterns
  • Repeat safe structures confidently

Computer-based grammar mistakes (hidden problem)

On computer-based IELTS, grammar mistakes increase due to typing + pressure.

What breaks accuracy

  • Fast typing without review
  • Auto-pilot sentence start
  • No time to re-check tense

What we train

  • Grammar-safe sentence patterns
  • Typing with control
  • Pressure-proof accuracy
