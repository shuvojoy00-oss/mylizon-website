Reference Charts (Course-wide)

These are global resources used across all classes and topics.
Connectives Master Chart Skill-wise

Speaking (Part 1 basic flow)

  • and (add) — I like tea and coffee.
  • but (contrast) — It’s hard but useful.
  • because (reason) — I like it because it helps me.
  • so (result) — I practiced, so I improved.
  • also (extra) — I also watch videos.

Writing Task 1 (data)

  • overall, whereas, while, in contrast, similarly, compared to

Writing Task 2 (argument)

  • however, therefore, as a result, moreover, furthermore, on the one hand / on the other hand

Reading signals

  • however (contrast coming), for example (example follows), overall (summary), finally (ending)

Listening signals

  • but, however, actually, instead, finally often indicate the real answer is near.
Grammar Application Chart Task-wise

Speaking

  • Part 1: Present + simple reasons (because) + usually/sometimes.
  • Part 2: Past for stories + time words (before/after/then).
  • Part 3: Degree (comparison), general present, simple passive (basic awareness).

Writing Task 1

  • Comparison (degree), passive, correct tense for years, linkers (whereas/while/in contrast).

Writing Task 2

  • General present, basic conditionals, degree, logical connectors (however/therefore).

Reading & Listening

  • Recognize signals: passive tone, comparisons, condition clauses, time phrases.

Teacher Area

This section is for teacher training + delivery rules. (The full consolidated handbook is pending packaging.)
ZERO Delivery Rules (Non-negotiable)
  • Confidence > accuracy. Don’t over-correct.
  • Students must speak. Teacher must not dominate.
  • Use the topic chart order: Read → Fill → Q/A → Expand.
  • Correction style: fix 1–2 key errors only (not every sentence).
  • Always model a short, natural answer first.
Teacher Materials Checklist
  • Printed topic charts (or open this link on phone)
  • Whiteboard/marker or notes app
  • Timer (to manage speaking turns)
  • Quick attendance + speaking participation check
