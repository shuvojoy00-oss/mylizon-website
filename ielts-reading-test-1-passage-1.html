The development of the London underground railway

In the first half of the 1800s, 1London’s population grew at an astonishing rate, and the central area became increasingly congested. In addition, the expansion of the overground railway network resulted in more and more passengers arriving in the capital. [cite: 90]

However, in 1846, a Royal Commission decided that the railways should not be allowed to enter the City, the capital’s historic and business centre. The result was that the overground railway stations formed a ring around the City. The area within consisted of poorly built, overcrowded slums and the streets were full of horse-drawn traffic. Crossing the City became a nightmare. [cite: 91, 92, 93, 94]

Amongst the most vocal advocates for a solution to London’s traffic problems was Charles Pearson. He saw both social and economic advantages in building an underground railway that would link the overground railway stations together and clear London slums at the same time. 2His idea was to relocate the poor workers who lived in the inner-city slums to newly constructed suburbs, and to provide cheap rail travel for them to get to work. Pearson’s ideas gained support amongst 3some businessmen and in 1851 he submitted a plan to Parliament. [cite: 96, 97, 98]

The Metropolitan Railway Company was established in 1854. 4The organisation had difficulty in raising the funding for such a radical and expensive scheme, 5not least because of the critical articles printed by the press. Objectors argued that the tunnels would collapse under the weight of traffic overhead. [cite: 99, 100]

The construction used a technique known as ‘cut and cover’. A trench was dug, brick walls constructed, and finally a brick arch was added to create a tunnel. 6A two-metre-deep layer of soil was laid on top of the tunnel and the road above rebuilt. [cite: 105, 107, 108]

The Metropolitan line, which opened on 10 January 1863, 7was the world’s first underground railway. On its first day, 8almost 40,000 passengers were carried, though no mention was made of predictions. smoke and fumes remained a problem, 9even though ventilation shafts were added to the tunnels. [cite: 109, 114]

By the 1880s, congestion on London’s streets had become worse. 10The ‘cut and cover’ method of construction was not an option in this part of the capital. The only alternative was to tunnel deep underground. [cite: 115, 117, 118]

The City & South London opened in 1890. 11The carriages were narrow and had tiny windows just below the roof because it was thought that passengers would not want to look out at the tunnel walls. 12Although the City & South London Railway was a great technical achievement, it did not make a profit. Regarding the ‘Tuppenny Tube’, 13it was very popular, but its punctuality is not mentioned. [cite: 121, 123, 124, 125]

Questions 7-13

TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN

7. Other countries built underground railways before the Metropolitan.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): FALSE. প্যাসেজে স্পষ্টভাবে উল্লেখ আছে যে মেট্রোপলিটন লাইন ছিল "world’s first underground railway" (বিশ্বের প্রথম ভূগর্ভস্থ রেলপথ)। এর আগে অন্য কোথাও এটি ছিল না।

8. More people than predicted travelled on the first day.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): NOT GIVEN. প্রথম দিনে প্রায় ৪০,০০০ যাত্রী যাতায়াত করেছিল তা বলা আছে, কিন্তু আগে থেকে কত যাত্রী হবে বলে predicted (পূর্বাভাস) দেওয়া হয়েছিল, সেই তথ্য প্যাসেজে নেই।

9. Ventilation shafts failed to prevent pollution.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): TRUE. প্যাসেজে বলা হয়েছে যে ভেন্টিলেশন শ্যাফট ব্যবহার করার পরেও ধোঁয়া এবং গ্যাস (smoke and fumes) একটি সমস্যা হিসেবে রয়ে গিয়েছিল। অর্থাৎ তারা দূষণ পুরোপুরি রোধ করতে ব্যর্থ হয়েছিল।

10. A different approach was required in central London.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): TRUE. সেন্ট্রাল লন্ডনে 'কাট অ্যান্ড কভার' পদ্ধতি সম্ভব ছিল না। সেখানে গভীর টানেল খোঁড়ার (tunnel deep underground) ভিন্ন পদ্ধতির প্রয়োজন ছিল।

11. Windows were at eye level.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): FALSE. প্যাসেজে বলা হয়েছে জানালাগুলো ছিল "tiny windows just below the roof" (ছাদের ঠিক নিচে খুব ছোট জানালা)। তাই এগুলো আই-লেভেল বা চোখের উচ্চতায় ছিল না।

12. The City & South London Railway made a profit.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): FALSE. এটি প্রযুক্তিগতভাবে সফল হলেও প্যাসেজে বলা হয়েছে "it did not make a profit" (এটি কোনো মুনাফা করতে পারেনি)।

13. 'Tuppenny Tube' nearly always ran on time.

ব্যাখ্যা (Explanation): NOT GIVEN. প্যাসেজে 'Tuppenny Tube' এর জনপ্রিয়তার (popular) কথা বলা হয়েছে, কিন্তু এটি সময়মতো চলত (punctuality) কি না তা নিয়ে কোনো তথ্য দেওয়া নেই।
