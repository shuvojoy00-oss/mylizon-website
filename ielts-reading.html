Why IELTS Reading feels hard (even after practice)

Most Bangladeshi students don’t fail IELTS Reading because English is weak. They fail because they translate in their head, read everything, and chase meaning instead of answers.

By the time you finish “understanding”, time is gone—and the answer is missed.

  • Slow reading speed
  • Panic under time pressure
  • Correct understanding, wrong answers

How high-scorers read differently

High scorers don’t read everything. They read with purpose. IELTS rewards strategy + control, not perfect English.

  • Questions first, then passage
  • Scan for signals, not sentences
  • Ignore unnecessary meaning
  • Trust structure more than vocabulary

How LizOn trains Reading (not just practices it)

At LizOn, Reading is trained in stages: we remove translation habits, build controlled scanning, train question-type logic, and finally test under real pressure with evaluation.

Reading improvement is engineered, not hoped for.
